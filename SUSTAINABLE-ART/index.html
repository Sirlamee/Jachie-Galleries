SUSTAINABLE ART HOUSE

The Sustainable Art House is a long-term vision imagined by Jachi Gallery a response to the growing realities of waste, lost narratives, and the lack of sustainable creative spaces across Africa. It begins as a dream, but one rooted deeply in lived experience.

We imagine a large, light-filled warehouse transformed into a living creative ecosystem. A place where discarded textiles are not seen as waste, but as material carrying history, memory, and possibility. Where artists work freely, surrounded by fabric, colour, and conversation.

Where creation is slow, intentional, and deeply connected to community. This space is envisioned as more than a stuido. It is a home for making, learning, and preserving, a place where African narratives are told through material, process, and collaboration.

WHAT SUSTAINABLE ART WILL BECOME

A shared studio space for textile-based and mixed-media artists

Learning and mentorship programs for young and emerging creatives

Workshops and community sessions centered on sustainability and material reuse

Archival and storytelling spaced that document African identity through art

Collaborative projects with sustainability leaders, institutions and global partners

The Sustainable Art House is not meant to be built alone. It is a call to sustainability advocates, cultural institutions, collectors, educators, and global organizations who believe in the power of art as a tool for change. If this vision resonates with you , if you can already picture the space, the people, the energy, we welcome conversation, collaboration, and shared imagination.
