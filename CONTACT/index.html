I am Solomon Chidinma, a Nigerian contemporary artist working primarily with discarded fabrics and garments. My practice is rooted in transformation ,turning what is overlooked, worn, or forgotten into works that speak of identity, resilience, and worth.

Fabric is not just my medium; it is my language. Every piece carries history of labor, culture, movement, and survival. Through cutting, layering, stitching, and reconstruction, I give these materials new life, allowing them to hold space as fine art while honoring where they come from. I studied Fine and Applied Arts at Alex Ekwueme Federal University, graduating in 2023, where I began refining my approach to material exploration and portraiture. My work is deeply influenced by African narratives, sustainability, and the quiet strength found in everyday materials.

At the core of my practice is intention. I create slowly and deliberately, believing that value is not determined by novelty, but by meaning. I am committed to building a future where African art is globally visible, environmentally conscious, and emotionally honest. Through my work, I seek to challenge perception, preserve stories, and reimagine what art and material can become. This is not just creation, it is reclamation, it is purpose.
